How the Algorithm Works for You

LinkedIn’s 2025 algorithm shift is the engine behind every recommendation in this deck

LinkedIn now distributes content by topic, beyond your network.

In 2025, LinkedIn shifted from a social graph (who follows you) to an interest graph (what people care about). Every post is classified into topic clusters and tested against audiences who engage with similar themes. For Bracewell, this means: nail 2–3 environmental topics consistently, and LinkedIn will surface your content to exactly the audience you want — even if they don’t follow you.

Algorithm Insight

Topic Consistency = Reach

Creators who repeat 2–3 core topics for 90 days see +27% avg reach and +41% topic-based follows. Bracewell’s five pillars are designed for exactly this signal: Regulatory PFAS ESG Energy Credibility

Algorithm Insight

Conversation Is the Currency

Comments, saves, and profile clicks now outweigh likes. Posts that inspire long comment threads stay visible for weeks via “Suggested for You” feeds. Saves alone increase resurfacing probability by 60%. Narrative-driven posts earn 2× the comments of link-promo.

Algorithm Insight

Teach > Promote

LinkedIn’s classifier favors content that educates over content that sells. Across the peer set, thought leadership posts drive 9.4× more engagement than link-promo posts. Hogan’s 122-word narrative TL vs. Bracewell’s 56-word link-promo tells the whole story.

The shift: From “Check out our latest alert” to “Here’s what the Endangerment Finding rescission actually means for your permits.” Same expertise, different packaging. The algorithm (and your audience) will reward the change immediately.

Six Signature Formats

Repeatable templates the marketing team can produce consistently. Algorithm Insight Document posts (carousels) earn +47% engagement and +23% reach. Text + Image is the most reliable anchor format (+16% engagement).

📜 The Regulatory Breakdown

120–180 words. Trend framing hook → what changed → what it means for [audience] → forward-looking implication. Narrative voice, no outbound link in body. Branded infographic or key stat visual.

🎤 The Conference Recap

100–150 words. “3 things we took away from [Event]” → numbered insights with 1–2 sentence explanations → closing thought or question. First-person voice (partner or team), observational.

Pillar 5

⚠️ The Industry Alert

80–120 words. Urgency hook → specific deadline or development → who’s affected → one concrete action step. Direct, practical, no jargon. Branded deadline graphic.

Pillar 2 Pillar 4

📊 The Carousel Deep-Dive

8–12 slides, 30–50 words/slide. Hook slide → problem framing → 4–6 data points → implication → CTA in comments. Vertical format (Canva). Best for comparisons, checklists, frameworks.

Pillar 2 Pillar 3 Pillar 4

👤 The Partner POV

100–160 words. Personal stake/observation → expert analysis in first person → forward-looking opinion → no CTA. Posted from individual partner profiles. Individual voice configured per brand voice profile.

📰 The Media Aggregator

60–80 words intro + 4–6 slide carousel. Brief framing intro → carousel slides with publication logo, headline, 1-line summary. Use when 4+ media hits land in same week on related topics.

Posting Rules

  • Minimum 100 words per thought leadership post (target 120–160)
  • Maximum 2 hashtags per post (event-specific only)
  • Links in first comment, never in the post body
  • Always include an image or carousel (3.9× engagement)
  • No repost-with-commentary as primary format
  • Conference content = recap, not promo
  • No politics — regulatory analysis and business impact only
  • No client event publicity — env practice clients too sensitive
  • Space same-source content 7+ days apart
  • Maximum 8 @mentions per post (likely to engage back)
  • AI content guardrail: Use AI for ideation and structure only. Add practitioner data, client scenarios, and specific analysis to every draft. Algorithm Insight Fully AI-generated posts get 2.8× less reach; AI-assisted with human edits performs at near-human levels
Algorithm Insight Month 1 A/B Tests: Post length (80–100 vs. 140–180 words) • Hook style (question-led vs. narrative framing) • Hashtag usage (0 vs. 2) • Link placement (body vs. first comment). 4 posts per variant, measured by engagement rate. Optimal posting frequency: 4×/week is sustainable with active engagement; minimum 18 hours between posts to avoid self-cannibalization.

The Engagement Engine

Posting is half the program. Page management, employee amplification, and strategic commenting are the distribution multipliers. Algorithm Insight Reply activity reduces reach decline by 14%.

Page Response Protocol

  • Reply to every comment within 2 hours during business hours (8 AM–6 PM CT)
  • First 90 minutes are critical — the algorithm evaluates post quality in this window. Pre-schedule responses for first-wave comments
  • Quality standard: Minimum 15 words per reply. Add insight, ask a follow-up question, or reference specific data
  • Escalation: Flag negative or legal-risk comments to Sally Slater within 1 hour. Do not delete or argue publicly
  • Tag partners: When a comment relates to a partner’s focus area, tag them in the reply to activate their network

Employee Amplification Program

  • Social HP platform: ~100 active users, 50% weekly participation. Target: 60% weekly within 90 days
  • 3–4 pre-drafted share options per company page post (varying angles and tones)
  • Partner-exclusive versions for Jason Hut, Seth Barsky, and event speakers (first-person voice)
  • Notification cadence: Slack/Teams alert within 30 minutes of each new post
  • Environmental practice attorneys are priority amplifiers — their shares carry topical authority
Algorithm Insight Company pages get significantly lower organic reach than individual profiles. Employee sharing is your primary distribution lever — each share exposes the post to a new personal network.

Strategic Engagement from Page

  • Comment as Bracewell on 3–5 industry posts per week (EPA announcements, trade association content, peer firm milestones)
  • Engage with partner posts from the company page within 30 minutes of publishing
  • A3 comment pattern: Add insight → Ask a specific question → Anchor with a takeaway. Minimum 25 words
  • Cross-cluster engagement: Comment on diverse profiles (energy, manufacturing, real estate, healthcare) to expand Bracewell’s topic graph
  • Avoid: Generic “Great post!” comments. Every comment should demonstrate environmental expertise
