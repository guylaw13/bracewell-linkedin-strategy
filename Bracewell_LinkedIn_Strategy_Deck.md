# SECTION 1: EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

---

## The Opportunity

Bracewell's Environmental practice produces **more environmental content than any competitor** (42% of all environmental posts across 4 peer firms) -- but engagement per post trails significantly.

The gap isn't volume. It's format, voice, and topic mix.

This strategy addresses three core objectives:
1. **Close the engagement gap** by shifting from link-promo to narrative-driven content
2. **Expand audience reach** by speaking to what C-suite, boards, and in-house counsel actually worry about
3. **Build a repeatable system** that scales beyond Environmental to other practices

**Pilot period:** February 2 -- May 2, 2026 (90 days)

---

## What Success Looks Like

| Metric | Baseline | 90-Day Target |
|--------|----------|---------------|
| Avg engagement per environmental post | 10.7 | 25+ |
| Avg word count per post | 56-93 words | 120-160 words |
| Content types used | 2 (TL + Awards) | 5+ (add Events, Deals, People) |
| Employee advocacy participation | ~100 active / 50% weekly | 120 active / 60% weekly |
| Environmental subthemes covered | 2 (Policy, Carbon) | 5+ (add ESG Strategy, Energy Transition, Renewables) |
| Partner thought leadership posts | Not tracked | 2-4 per month (Jason Hut, Seth Barsky, +1) |

**Qualitative success signals:**
- Lawyers report feeling their work is visible and valued
- BD leads generated through thought leadership (modeled on Arbitration Acumen podcast success)
- Increased website referral traffic to partner bios and Environmental practice pages
- Positive internal feedback loop driving more partner participation

## Six Signature Formats

Each format is a repeatable template. The marketing team can produce these consistently using AI workflows.

### Format 1: The Regulatory Breakdown (Pillar 1, 2, 3)
- **Length:** 120-180 words
- **Structure:** Trend framing hook (1-2 sentences) > What changed (2-3 sentences) > What it means for [specific audience] (2-3 sentences) > Forward-looking implication (1 sentence)
- **Voice:** Authoritative, narrative, no link in body (link in comments if needed)
- **Image:** Branded infographic or key stat visual
- **Example hook:** "The EPA rescinded the Endangerment Finding last month. But your existing permits? Still in effect. Here's why that distinction matters..."

### Format 2: The Conference Recap (Pillar 5)
- **Length:** 100-150 words
- **Structure:** "3 things we took away from [Event Name]" > Numbered insights with 1-2 sentence explanations each > Closing thought or question
- **Voice:** First-person (partner or team), observational, insight-driven
- **Image:** Event photo (if available) or branded graphic with event name
- **Example hook:** "Just got back from [Conference]. Three things that stuck with us from the environmental track..."

### Format 3: The Industry Alert (Pillar 2, 4)
- **Length:** 80-120 words
- **Structure:** Urgency hook > Specific deadline or development > Who's affected > One concrete action step
- **Voice:** Direct, practical, no jargon
- **Image:** Branded deadline or alert graphic
- **Example hook:** "If your company has manufactured, imported, or processed PFAS since 2011, you have a reporting obligation starting April 2026. Here's what that means..."

### Format 4: The Carousel Deep-Dive (Pillar 2, 3, 4)
- **Length:** 8-12 slides, 30-50 words per slide
- **Structure:** Hook slide > Problem framing > 4-6 key data points or insights > Implication slide > Closing CTA (in comments)
- **Voice:** Clean, data-driven, skimmable
- **Template:** Vertical format per new LinkedIn sizing (Canva template required)
- **Best for:** State-by-state comparisons, PFAS industry breakdowns, compliance checklists, audit frameworks

### Format 5: The Partner POV (Pillar 1, 3, 5)
- **Length:** 100-160 words
- **Structure:** Personal stake or observation > Expert analysis in first person > Forward-looking opinion > No CTA
- **Voice:** Individual partner voice (configured per brand voice profile)
- **Posted from:** Individual partner LinkedIn profiles (not company page)
- **Example hook:** "I've spent the last 20 years advising energy companies on environmental compliance. What's happening right now with the Endangerment Finding is unlike anything I've seen..."

### Format 6: The Media Aggregator (Pillar 5)
- **Length:** 60-80 words intro + carousel of 4-6 media hits
- **Structure:** Brief intro framing the theme > Carousel slides with publication logo, headline, and 1-line summary each
- **Voice:** Proud but not boastful
- **When to use:** When 4+ media hits land in the same week on related topics
- **Example hook:** "It's been a busy week for our Environmental team in the press. Here's what we've been weighing in on..."

---

## Posting Rules

1. **Never post 3 articles from the same source in the same week.** Space repurposed content across 7+ days
2. **Maximum 2 hashtags per post.** Reserve for event-specific discoverability only
3. **Maximum 8 @mentions per post.** Tag people/orgs likely to engage back
4. **Always include an image or carousel.** Posts with images generate 3.9x more engagement
5. **Links go in the first comment, not the post body.** LinkedIn's algorithm deprioritizes outbound links
6. **No repost-with-commentary as the primary format.** Write fresh posts instead
7. **Minimum 100 words per thought leadership post.** Target 120-160 words
8. **Conference content = recap, not promo.** Pre-event posts are fine but recaps drive engagement
9. **No political positioning.** Stick to regulatory analysis and business impact. Maintain neutral stance for policy influence work
10. **No client event publicity.** Environmental practice clients are too sensitive for this

For every piece of environmental content, follow this sequence:

```
1. TRIGGER: New alert, white paper, media hit, conference, or regulatory development arrives

   - Alert/article → Article Distribution workflow (3 post angles + employee advocacy variations)
   - Announcement → News Amplifier workflow (company + advocacy posts)
   - Partner insight → LinkedIn Viralyzer (individual thought leader post variations)

3. REVIEW: Marketing team edits for accuracy, tone, and compliance with posting rules

4. APPROVE: Partner approval where required (especially individual posts)

5. SCHEDULE: Place in HubSpot calendar per cadence recommendations
   - Space articles from the same source 7+ days apart
   - Balance across 5 pillars weekly
   - Don't stack more than 3 environmental posts in a single day across all channels

6. AMPLIFY: Load employee advocacy versions into Social HP
```
